# Context Profiles Reference

Weight calibration frameworks by seniority level, function type, and company type.

## Seniority Profiles

### Entry-Level (0-3 years experience)

**Weight Distribution:**
| Criterion | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Education | 40% | Primary capability proxy |
| Hard Skills | 35% | Technical foundation |
| Internships/Projects | 15% | Experience substitute |
| Soft Skill Signals | 10% | Initiative, learning speed |

**Content Strategy:**
- Education: PROMINENT (GPA if >3.5, relevant coursework, honors)
- Skills: COMPREHENSIVE list—breadth matters
- Projects: Feature as primary experience; avoid cookie-cutter (Titanic, MNIST)
- Achievements: Emphasize potential, learning speed, initiative over outcomes

**Summary Template:**
```
Recent [Degree] graduate from [Institution] with [internship/project] experience 
in [domain]. Developed [specific skill] through [specific project/course]. 
Eager to apply [JD keyword 1] and [JD keyword 2] skills to [target role type].
```

---

### Mid-Career (3-10 years experience)

**Weight Distribution:**
| Criterion | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Hard Skills | 40% | Must demonstrate proficiency |
| Experience Relevance | 25% | Direct applicability |
| Career Progression | 20% | Growth trajectory |
| Achievements | 15% | Impact evidence |

**Content Strategy:**
- Education: BRIEF (degree, school, year only—no GPA, no coursework)
- Skills: TARGETED to role requirements (not comprehensive dump)
- Experience: LEAD with achievements, not duties
- Progression: EMPHASIZE title advancement, scope expansion

**Summary Template:**
```
[Title] with [X] years driving [specific outcome type] in [industry/domain]. 
At [Current/Recent Company], [top quantified achievement]. Expert in [JD keyword 1], 
[JD keyword 2], and [JD keyword 3]. Known for [soft skill demonstrated by achievement].
```

---

### Senior/Executive (10+ years, leadership roles)

**Weight Distribution:**
| Criterion | Weight | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Strategic Impact | 30% | Vision and business outcomes |
| Leadership Evidence | 25% | Team/org transformation |
| Executive Presence | 20% | Board, C-suite engagement |
| Domain Expertise | 20% | Credibility foundation |
| Education | 5% | Checkbox only |

**Content Strategy:**
- Education: MINIMAL (may omit unless prestigious/required)
- Strategic impact: LEAD every section—transformation over execution
- P&L/Board exposure: FEATURE prominently
- Scale indicators: Enterprise-wide, global, company-wide

**Summary Template:**
```
[Executive title] who [signature transformation achievement]. Built/led [scope: 
org size, budget, geography] delivering [business outcome with metrics]. 
Trusted advisor to [C-suite/Board] on [strategic domain]. Brings [unique capability] 
to drive [value proposition aligned to JD].
```

## Function Profiles

### Technical Roles

**Evaluation Mode:** Boolean skills checklist + architecture judgment + scale evidence

**Must Include:**
- Specific technology versions (Python 3.11, not just "Python")
- Production scale metrics (users, transactions, uptime)
- Architecture decisions with tradeoff reasoning
- Unique projects (NOT: Titanic, MNIST, Twitter sentiment, to-do apps)

**Must Avoid:**
- Soft/marketing language ("passionate about technology")
- Vague scope ("large-scale systems" without numbers)
- Tutorial projects without unique angle

**Skills Section Format:**
```
**Languages:** Python 3.x, Go, TypeScript, SQL
**Cloud/Infrastructure:** GCP (Vertex AI, BigQuery, Cloud Run), AWS (Lambda, S3)
**Data/ML:** TensorFlow 2.x, PyTorch, LangChain, RAG pipelines
**Tools:** Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, Git, CI/CD (GitHub Actions)
```

---

### Sales/Revenue Roles

**Evaluation Mode:** Quota attainment primary, HIGH skepticism warranted

**Must Include:**
- % to quota (not just revenue closed)
- Deal metrics: ACV, cycle length, win rate
- Territory/segment specification
- Hunter vs. Farmer classification clarity

**Credibility Markers:**
- Specific quota numbers and attainment percentages
- Named accounts (when permitted)
- Competitive wins with context

**Warning:** 78% of sales resumes contain misleading statements. Verify all claims are defensible.

**Metrics Format:**
```
- Achieved 127% of $2.4M annual quota; ranked #2 of 45 reps in Central Region
- Closed 23 enterprise deals ($150K+ ACV) with 34% win rate vs. 22% team average
- Expanded strategic account revenue 47% YoY through cross-sell into 3 new divisions
```

---

### Operations/Manufacturing Roles

**Evaluation Mode:** Operational metrics + certifications + crisis evidence

**Must Include:**
- OEE, yield, throughput, cycle time metrics
- Lean/Six Sigma certifications (feature prominently)
- Safety record and mindset signals
- Team/shift/facility scope

**Valued Signals:**
- Crisis problem-solving examples
- Union relations experience
- Scale: shifts managed, headcount, facility size
- Continuous improvement evidence

**Metrics Format:**
```
- Improved OEE from 72% to 89% across 3 production lines through TPM implementation
- Achieved 2.1M hours without lost-time incident; led plant to OSHA VPP Star status
- Managed 24/7 operation with 147 FTEs across 4 shifts; reduced turnover 34%
```

---

### Executive/Leadership Roles

**Evaluation Mode:** "Whole Person" assessment (Capability, Character, Compatibility, Capacity)

**Must Include:**
- P&L responsibility with scale
- Board/C-suite engagement examples
- Transformation narratives (from-state → to-state)
- Team building/org development

**Assessment Dimensions:**
| Dimension | Evidence Type |
|-----------|---------------|
| Capability | Track record of outcomes at scale |
| Character | Values alignment, ethical leadership signals |
| Compatibility | Culture fit indicators, leadership style |
| Capacity | Growth trajectory, learning agility |

**Transformation Format:**
```
- Transformed [function] from [problem state] to [outcome state], delivering 
  [metric] improvement over [timeframe]
- Built [capability/team] from ground up; grew organization from [X] to [Y], 
  now generating [business impact]
```

## Company Type Profiles

### Startup (<50 employees)

**Signals to Emphasize:**
- "Wore multiple hats" / cross-functional experience
- Speed and agility examples
- Scrappy resourcefulness
- Risk tolerance / comfort with ambiguity
- Zero-to-one building experience

**Signals to De-emphasize:**
- Heavy enterprise pedigree (may signal inability to operate without structure)
- Deep specialization (may signal inability to flex)
- Process-heavy experience

**Startup-Friendly Phrases:**
- "Built from scratch" / "Stood up" / "Launched"
- "Operated across functions"
- "Moved fast while maintaining quality"
- "Made decisions with incomplete information"

---

### Scale-up (50-500 employees)

**Signals to Emphasize:**
- Growth trajectory / "distance traveled"
- Scaling experience (grew team from X to Y)
- Process creation (not just following)
- Adaptability to rapid change
- Execution speed with quality

**Valued Pattern:**
Candidates who've successfully transitioned from startup chaos to structured growth.

---

### Enterprise (500+ employees)

**Signals to Emphasize:**
- Brand recognition (known companies)
- Deep specialization
- Longer tenures (3+ years per role)
- Clear advancement path
- Cross-functional collaboration at scale
- Governance/compliance experience

**Signals to De-emphasize:**
- Frequent job changes
- "Startup mentality" language (may signal inability to work within structure)
- Generalist positioning

**Enterprise-Friendly Phrases:**
- "Led cross-functional initiative across X business units"
- "Partnered with [function] to deliver enterprise-wide [outcome]"
- "Established governance framework for [domain]"
- "Scaled program from pilot to global rollout"

---

## Context Combination Matrix

When multiple contexts apply, use this priority order:

1. **Seniority level** (determines overall structure and content depth)
2. **Function type** (determines skills emphasis and metrics focus)
3. **Company type** (determines language and signal selection)

**Example:** Senior Technical role at Startup
- Lead with strategic technical impact (senior)
- Include specific tech stack and scale metrics (technical)
- Emphasize building/launching and adaptability (startup)
- De-emphasize process and governance (startup)

**Example:** Mid-career Sales role at Enterprise
- Balance experience and progression (mid-career)
- Lead with quota attainment and deal metrics (sales)
- Emphasize known accounts and stable tenure (enterprise)
- Include cross-functional collaboration examples (enterprise)
