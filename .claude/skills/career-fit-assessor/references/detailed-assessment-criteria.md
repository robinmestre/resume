# Detailed Assessment Criteria

Complete methodology for comprehensive career fit analysis with weighted Critical Success Factor evaluation.

## Phase 1: Job Description Deep Analysis

### 1.1 Surface-Level Extraction
- Explicitly stated required skills and qualifications
- Explicitly stated preferred skills and qualifications
- Years of experience requirements
- Educational requirements
- Technical certifications or credentials

### 1.2 Between-the-Lines Analysis
- What's emphasized vs. mentioned in passing (frequency, position, detail level)
- Company context and culture signals (language, values, tone)
- Role positioning within organization (reporting structure, scope)
- Implicit technical stack or methodology preferences
- Red flags or unusual requirements

### 1.3 Industry Context Research
- Success factors common to similar roles at peer companies
- Industry-standard competencies not explicitly listed
- Emerging skills increasingly expected in this role type
- Compensation and seniority benchmarking signals

## Phase 2: Critical Success Factor Development

### 2.1 CSF Categories

**Technical/Hard Skills** (typically 3-5 factors)
- Core technical competencies
- Tool and platform proficiency
- Methodological expertise
- Domain knowledge requirements

**Experience & Track Record** (typically 2-4 factors)
- Years and depth of relevant experience
- Scope and scale of prior work
- Industry or domain experience
- Leadership and team experience

**Soft Skills & Competencies** (typically 2-3 factors)
- Communication and stakeholder management
- Problem-solving approach
- Collaboration and influence
- Adaptability and learning agility

**Cultural & Strategic Fit** (typically 1-2 factors)
- Values alignment with company
- Career trajectory alignment with role
- Work style compatibility
- Strategic thinking and vision

### 2.2 Weighting Guidelines

| Weight | Meaning | Criteria |
|--------|---------|----------|
| 9-10 | Must-have | Role cannot be performed without this; deal-breaker if absent |
| 7-8 | Critical | Strongly differentiates top candidates; hard to train |
| 5-6 | Important | Expected competency; trainable within 6 months |
| 3-4 | Preferred | Nice-to-have; trainable within onboarding |
| 1-2 | Minor | Marginal advantage; rarely decisive |

## Phase 3: Candidate Assessment

### 3.1 Fit Scoring Guidelines

| Score | Meaning | Evidence Required |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| 9-10 | Exceptional | Exceeds requirement; proven at higher level |
| 7-8 | Strong | Meets requirement with clear evidence; recent and relevant |
| 5-6 | Adequate | Meets minimum; some gaps or staleness |
| 3-4 | Developing | Partial match; transferable but indirect |
| 1-2 | Weak | Minimal evidence; significant training needed |
| 0 | Absent | No relevant experience or skills |

### 3.2 Evidence Hierarchy
1. **Direct recent experience** (highest): Same skill/responsibility in last 3 years
2. **Direct historical experience**: Same skill/responsibility 3+ years ago
3. **Adjacent transferable experience**: Related skill in similar context
4. **Demonstrated learning ability**: Rapid acquisition of similar skills
5. **Theoretical knowledge only** (lowest): Education or certification without application

## Phase 4: Red Team/Blue Team Deep Analysis

### Blue Team Protocol
For each CSF, answer:
- What is the strongest evidence from career history?
- How does candidate exceed typical applicants?
- What unique perspective or differentiator exists?
- How would you defend this candidate to a skeptical hiring manager?

### Red Team Protocol
For each CSF, answer:
- What specific evidence would a competitor likely have that this candidate lacks?
- What questions would expose this gap in an interview?
- What concerns would a hiring manager raise?
- What's the honest assessment of competitive position?

## Phase 5: Passion Alignment Assessment

### Evaluation Criteria
- **Interest alignment**: Does role content match stated interests?
- **Growth alignment**: Does role enable desired skill development?
- **Impact alignment**: Does role enable type of impact candidate seeks?
- **Lifestyle alignment**: Does role fit desired work patterns?
- **Identity alignment**: Does role fit candidate's professional self-concept?

### Passion-Fit Matrix
| Skill Fit | Passion Fit | Recommendation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| High | High | Strong pursue |
| High | Low | Pursue with caution; burnout risk |
| Low | High | Stretch opportunity; higher risk/reward |
| Low | Low | Pass |
